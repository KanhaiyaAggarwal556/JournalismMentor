Why Journalism Is Important?

1. Informs the public about current events, issues, and developments, enabling informed decision-making.

2. Holds individuals and institutions accountable for their actions, promoting transparency and integrity.

3. Fosters democracy by providing diverse viewpoints, fostering public debate, and ensuring government transparency.

4. Advocates for human rights by exposing abuses, raising awareness, and mobilizing public opinion for positive change.

5. Preserves history, culture, and societal changes through storytelling and documentation, contributing to collective memory and heritage preservation.

6. Journalism exposes hidden agendas, corporate interests, and government policies, promoting transparency and accountability in decision-making processes.
